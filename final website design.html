About Us

Gamexia is an after-school activity program designed to make learning more fun and engaging, improving retention and understanding.

Dyslexia is a neurobiological condition that affects 1 in 5 children worldwide. It causes difficulties with accurate and fluent word recognition, spelling, and decoding. Traditional learning environments often fail to meet their needs.

For most literate individuals, reading happens automatically. A popular internet message illustrates how easily people read even when letters are scrambled:

"It deosn’t mttaer in waht oredr the ltteers in a wrod are, the olny iprmoetnt tihng is taht the frist and lsat ltteer be at the rghit pclae. The rset can be a toatl mses and you can sitll raed it wouthit porbelm."

Dyslexia disrupts this automatic process at a fundamental level, and that is where GAMEXIA comes in.

Our afterschool program provides a structured, engaging, and personalized learning experience that helps bridge this gap.

Why After-School Activities?

  • Engaging and Multisensory Learning – Games integrate visual, auditory, and tactile elements, helping dyslexic children process information in multiple ways.
  • Personalized Support – Activities can be tailored to each child’s needs, providing them with the right level of challenge and support.
  • Improved Focus and Confidence – Interactive games motivate children to stay engaged and build self-esteem through success and progression.
  • Teacher & Parent Dashboards – Provide real-time data and progress reports to parents and teachers, giving them actionable insights and strategies to better support the child’s learning journey.
  • Peer-Based Learning and Community – Students can interact, collaborate, and learn from one another in a safe, encouraging space, helping to combat isolation and promote teamwork.

Teacher's Training

Teacher training is crucial in supporting dyslexic children, as it equips educators with the skills to create an inclusive and effective learning environment. Here’s why it matters:

  • Early Identification & Intervention – Trained teachers can spot early signs of dyslexia and provide timely, targeted support.
  • Multisensory Teaching Techniques – Effective training introduces methods like Orton-Gillingham and structured literacy to enhance learning.
  • Boosting Student Confidence – With the right strategies, teachers can reduce frustration, foster self-esteem, and make learning enjoyable.
  • Adapting Curriculum & Assessments – Teachers learn how to modify lessons, use assistive technology, and offer alternative evaluation methods.
  • Parental & Peer Collaboration – Training encourages stronger communication between teachers, parents, and specialists to ensure consistent support.

Empowered teachers create empowered learners, making training a key pillar in dyslexia-friendly education.
